Major arrhythmias in non-dilated left ventricular cardiomyopathy: a novel prediction score.
European heart journal · 2026-01-05 · Peretto G, Merlo M, Ambrosi A
Study Type
Retrospective cohort
Finding
In a retrospective cohort of 337 patients with non-dilated left ventricular cardiomyopathy, male sex (HR 2.4, 95% CI 1.3-4.4, P = .007), baseline non-sustained ventricular tachycardia (HR 3.1, 95% CI 1.7-5.6, P < .001), LVEF < 45% (HR 5.5, 95% CI 2.7-11.0, P < .001), septal LGE (HR 2.0, 95% CI 1.0-4.0, P = .046), pathogenic variants in high-risk genes (HR 4.6, 95% CI 2.3-9.1, P < .001), and myocardial inflammation (HR 15.7, 95% CI 6.1-40.3, P < .001) predicted the first major arrhythmic event by 60 months; these findings were validated in an external cohort with a C-index of 0.81.
So What?
This novel risk score incorporating clinical, imaging, genetic, and inflammatory markers can help clinicians stratify arrhythmic risk in patients with non-dilated left ventricular cardiomyopathy to guide monitoring and therapeutic decisions.

Randomized Controlled Trial of Mechanical Thrombectomy With Anticoagulation Versus Anticoagulation Alone for Acute Intermediate-High Risk Pulmonary Embolism: Primary Outcomes From the STORM-PE Trial.RCT
Circulation · 2025-11-03 · Lookstein R, Konstantinides S, Weinberg I
Study Type
RCT
Finding
Mechanical thrombectomy with anticoagulation (CAVT) reduced the RV/LV ratio by 0.52±0.37 compared to 0.24±0.40 with anticoagulation alone at 48 hours, with a difference of 0.27 (95% CI, 0.12-0.43; P<0.001). Early normalization of vital signs was more frequent with CAVT, and major adverse event rates within 7 days were similar between groups (4.3% vs 7.5%; P=0.681).
So What?
Mechanical thrombectomy plus anticoagulation improves early right ventricular remodeling and vital sign normalization without increasing short-term adverse events, supporting its use in intermediate-high risk pulmonary embolism management.

Frailty Scale Captures Multidimensional Vulnerability and Predicts Mortality in Heart Failure.
Journal of the American College of Cardiology · 2025-10-15 · Nakade T, Fujimoto Y, Singh Y
Study Type
Prospective cohort
Finding
In a prospective cohort of 3,905 hospitalized heart failure patients, higher Clinical Frailty Scale (CFS) scores were associated with progressively worse physical and cognitive function and significantly increased 2-year all-cause mortality (18.6% overall), with CFS improving mortality risk discrimination beyond physical and cognitive tests alone.
So What?
Clinicians can use the Clinical Frailty Scale as a practical, integrative tool to identify multidimensional vulnerability and better predict mortality risk in heart failure patients, aiding in risk stratification and management decisions.

Serum magnesium and outcomes in heart failure with reduced ejection fraction: the GALACTIC-HF trial.RCT
European heart journal · 2026-01-05 · Chimura M, Docherty K, Jhund P
Study Type
RCT
Finding
In the GALACTIC-HF randomized controlled trial involving 6147 outpatients with HFrEF, 10.7% had hypermagnesaemia, which was associated with a higher incidence rate of the primary composite outcome (34.9 per 100 person-years, 95% CI 31.2-39.0) compared to normal magnesium levels (20.9, 19.9-22.0); hypomagnesaemia was not associated with increased risk, and abnormal magnesium levels did not increase sudden death or ventricular tachyarrhythmia risk.
So What?
Clinicians should note that hypermagnesaemia in HFrEF patients signals higher risk of worsening HF or cardiovascular death, while hypomagnesaemia may not require routine correction, informing risk stratification and management decisions.

Withdrawal of heart failure therapy after atrial fibrillation rhythm control with ejection fraction normalization: the WITHDRAW-AF trial.RCT
European heart journal · 2026-01-07 · Segan L, Kistler P, Nanayakkara S
Study Type
RCT
Finding
In patients with atrial fibrillation-mediated cardiomyopathy and normalized LVEF after AF rhythm control, withdrawal of heart failure therapy did not significantly reduce LVEF at 6 months compared to continued therapy (LVEF ≥50% maintained in 90% vs 100%, OR 1.18, 95% CI 0.27-2.82, P = .47). Secondary measures including cardiac remodeling, biomarkers, functional status, quality of life, and arrhythmia recurrence were similar on versus off therapy.
So What?
Clinicians may consider withdrawing heart failure therapy in patients with AFCM who have normalized LVEF after AF rhythm control without compromising cardiac function over 6 months, potentially reducing medication burden.

Effects of Intensive Blood Pressure Control in Patients With Frailty: A Post Hoc Analysis From ESPRIT.RCT
Journal of the American College of Cardiology · 2025-10-15 · Li S, Peng Y, Li Y
Study Type
RCT
Finding
In a post hoc analysis of the ESPRIT trial including 11,255 hypertensive patients, intensive blood pressure control targeting systolic BP <120 mm Hg reduced major adverse cardiovascular events similarly across nonfrail, moderately frail, and severely frail groups, with risk ratios around 0.83-0.86 and no significant interaction by frailty status (Pinteraction=0.67). Safety outcomes did not differ by frailty status despite higher baseline risk of serious adverse events in frail patients.
So What?
Clinicians can consider intensive blood pressure lowering to <120 mm Hg in hypertensive patients regardless of frailty status, as benefits on cardiovascular outcomes are consistent and safety profiles do not worsen with frailty.

Fish-Oil Supplementation and Cardiovascular Events in Patients Receiving Hemodialysis.RCT
The New England journal of medicine · 2025-11-07 · Lok C, Farkouh M, Hemmelgarn B
Study Type
RCT
Finding
Daily supplementation with fish oil (4 g of n-3 polyunsaturated fatty acids) in patients receiving maintenance hemodialysis significantly reduced the rate of serious cardiovascular events compared to placebo (hazard ratio 0.57; 95% CI, 0.47 to 0.70; P<0.001). Secondary outcomes including cardiac death, myocardial infarction, peripheral vascular disease leading to amputation, stroke, and first cardiovascular event or death from any cause were also significantly lower in the fish-oil group.
So What?
This trial provides evidence that n-3 fatty acid supplementation can reduce serious cardiovascular events in hemodialysis patients, suggesting a potential preventive strategy in this high-risk population where effective therapies are limited.

Evolocumab in Patients without a Previous Myocardial Infarction or Stroke.RCT
The New England journal of medicine · 2025-11-08 · Bohula E, Marston N, Bhatia A
Study Type
RCT
Finding
Evolocumab reduced the risk of 3-point MACE (hazard ratio 0.75, 95% CI 0.65 to 0.86, P<0.001) and 4-point MACE (hazard ratio 0.81, 95% CI 0.73 to 0.89, P<0.001) compared to placebo in patients with atherosclerosis or diabetes without prior myocardial infarction or stroke over a median follow-up of 4.6 years.
So What?
This trial supports the use of evolocumab for primary prevention of cardiovascular events in high-risk patients without previous myocardial infarction or stroke, expanding the indication for PCSK9 inhibition beyond secondary prevention.
